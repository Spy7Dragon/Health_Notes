BLUE LIGHT BLOCKING

DESCRIPTION

Blue light blocks the production of melatonin. Blue light comes from the sun during the day and from artificial lighting and electronics during the night. Melatonin is a hormone that allows people to feel sleepy at night. Melatonin keeps a functional circadian rythym in animals. People are able to implement various strategies in order to use artificial lighting at night without disturbing melatonin production. The most common strategies are orange glasses, orange artificial lights, and orange tinted computer screens. A more strict strategy involves removing and physically blocking all artificial lighting.

USAGE

Block out blue light atleast three hours before desired sleep time.
Don't block out blue light if staying awake is priority.
